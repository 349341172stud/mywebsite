Favourite Food

Khoresh Karafs

Why It Matters

Khoresh Karafs is, without a doubt, my favourite food. I share a myriad of memories with this meal, especially sharing dinner with my family while eating it. Among all the foods that I am accustomed to eating, primarily Persian dishes with the sole exception of pasta, Khoresh Karafs possesses the most complex taste composition, putting it higher in my rankings.

However, the connections I have to precious memories and fleeting moments are the essential reason for my ranking of it among my favourite foods. It is quintessential to the overall make-up of Persian cuisine, and the development of the Persian taste palette is impossible to overlook. To me, and millions of Iranians across the globe, this meal serves as a sort of comfort, reminding us of home.

One precious memory I had in my childhood was injuring my knee. It was as if I was incapable. I was frustrated, and it felt as if the world had wantonly taken the precious epidermal layer of my patella. I rushed towards the general direction of my estate, slamming the door open—there was Khoresh Karafs. As I ate, my maternal guardian provided essential aid to my knee. Now, as I eat it, I experience a slightly concerning nostalgic flashback, with memories of that day rushing back.
